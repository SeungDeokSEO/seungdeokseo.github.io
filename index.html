Hello! I am a Ph.D. student in Political Science at the University of Illinois Urbana–Champaign. My research examines how foreign aid structures political incentives and organizational behavior within civil society, with implications for civic culture in aid-dependent contexts, particularly in the Middle East.

For my dissertation, I analyze the conditions under which NGOs cooperate or compete, highlighting organizational identity as a key mechanism shaping inter-NGO cooperation and competition.

Drawing on evidence from Jordan, I employ a mixed-methods research design that combines network analysis, surveys and experiments, and qualitative interviews to identify the mechanisms underlying inter-NGO cooperation and competition.

In 2024–25, I was an Illinois Global Institute Language Fellow (Arabic).
