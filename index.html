I am a Ph.D. student in Political Science (Comparative Politics) at the University of Illinois Urbana–Champaign. My research examines how foreign aid structures political incentives and organizational behavior within civil society, with implications for civic culture in aid-dependent contexts, particularly in the Middle East. I study inter-organizational relations among NGOs, focusing on the conditions under which organizations cooperate or compete.

Current Research

My dissertation analyzes the conditions under which NGOs cooperate or compete in aid-dependent environments, emphasizing organizational identity as a key mechanism shaping inter-organizational relations.

Drawing on evidence from Jordan, I employ a mixed-methods research design combining network analysis, surveys and experiments, and qualitative interviews to identify the mechanisms underlying inter-NGO cooperation and competition.
